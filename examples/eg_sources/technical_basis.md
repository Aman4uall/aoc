Ethylene glycol (MEG) is a bulk petrochemical intermediate used mainly in polyester, PET resin, coolants, solvents, and industrial formulations.

Key property note:
- Molecular weight: 62.07 g/mol
- Melting point: about -12.9 C
- Boiling point: about 197.3 C
- Density at ambient conditions: about 1.113 g/cm3
- Fully miscible with water

Industrial route note:
- The dominant industrial route is hydration of ethylene oxide with excess water.
- High water dilution suppresses formation of diethylene glycol and triethylene glycol.
- Major design burden shifts from reaction feasibility to heat removal, water recycle, and purification.

Hazard note:
- Ethylene oxide is highly flammable, toxic, and reactive.
- Ethylene glycol product handling is less severe but hot glycol service still requires containment and burn protection.
